Our Story

Where Everyone Has a Place

A journey built on two decades of teaching experience, a passion for child-centred education, and a genuine desire to serve our community.

Meet Our Owners

A Vision Rooted in Purpose

My name is Fathima Ashiqua, and together with my husband, Hafeez Nizham, I took over Little Village Preschool with a clear and purposeful vision: to strengthen quality, continuity, and outcomes for every child and family in our community.

My journey in education began in 2004, and over the past two decades I have had the privilege of teaching across the Middle East (Saudi Arabia and Dubai) and New Zealand, working in early childhood and primary education. I am a registered teacher and have also held leadership roles as a team leader and section head. These experiences have shaped my deep understanding of how children learn, grow, and transition through different stages of their educational journey.

Our vision of "where everyone has a place" guides everything we do. — Fathima Ashiqua, Owner

I came to New Zealand to pursue my master's in education and was drawn to the country's inclusive values and strong commitment to child-centred education. My teaching practice is guided by Te Whāriki, alongside educational approaches such as Montessori principles and Lev Vygotsky's emphasis on learning through relationships and social interaction.

Having worked closely within primary education, I clearly identified a gap in how children are supported during the transition from early learning into school. Understanding what primary teachers are looking for, I have strengthened a purposeful transition-to-school programme that supports children to become confident, independent, and self-regulated learners, both academically and socially.

The decision to take over Little Village Preschool was grounded in a genuine desire to serve our community.

FA
Fathima Ashiqua Owner
Our Foundation

Vision, Mission & Philosophy

The principles that shape every decision, every programme, and every interaction at Little Village Preschool.

Our Vision
Where everyone has a place.

A community where every child, family, and educator feels valued, supported, and empowered — guided by curiosity, creativity, and genuine belonging.

Our Mission

We are committed to providing a nurturing and inclusive environment where each child is supported to reach their full potential through curiosity, creativity, and play. We foster confident learners who feel valued, supported, and empowered in their learning journey.

Our Philosophy

At Little Village Preschool, we believe children thrive when they feel safe, respected, and nurtured. We support the development of the whole child by providing a warm, inclusive, and responsive environment that promotes emotional, social, physical, and cognitive wellbeing.

Our approach values play, exploration, and relationships as the foundation for learning. Inspired by Te Whāriki and child-centred, nature-based approaches, we encourage curiosity, creativity, independence, and safe risk-taking.

Strong partnerships with whānau, respect for diversity, and a commitment to inclusion underpin our practice. We recognise each child as a capable learner with their own unique way of knowing, being, and doing.

Community & Whānau

Our strength comes from our collective. Families, educators, and children working together to create a village where every child belongs and every voice is heard.

"Ehara taku toa i te toa takitahi, engari he toa takitini." — My strength is not that of an individual, but that of the collective.
